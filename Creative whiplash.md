It has been 55 days since I last posted a new article to Substack. 

Which, for the last 20 of them, have been almost like a ceiling that slowly descends. Intent on crushing me with a massive amount of pressure. 

But, my hands have been far from idle. I have read more fiction. Allowing myself to journey in alternate worlds. Where my mind is allowed to create ghastly and beautiful images of another's creation. They have relearned the shapes required to define chords on the neck of a guitar. Strengthened by callouses that have laid dormant for a time unknown to their bearer, me. 

In the cases I thought it was good enough, I shared those musical creations with a talented friend.  One who is a much better composer than I am. 

And despite being far from idle, this enormous weight kept inching it's way closer. Fractions at a time. 

Then, I opened up another book. 

Only, the fear of the slow descension of weight seemed to guide me to non-fiction.

Where I found the door that led me out from under the slowly lowering weight above. 

___

Not even out of the introduction of this new title yet, I discovered something about the core idea of 'child-like wonder' used as a guide in the zettlekasten method (the method I use in creating and curating ideas such as this one).
This ability allows the user to make connections in a way that seem outlandish initially. But when connected to other ideas leads to true, properly weighted self-disovery.

I built the walls, the roof and even the mechanics that allowed my fear to exist. Believing I must abandon one passion for another.

The introduction I read reviewed several scientific studies that showed the most elite athletes on the planet, in any sport, don't start to specialize until later in life than those who are described as "near elite". 

And that's when I came to this realization. 

I'm not abondoning one in favor of another.

I'm allowing myself to be pulled naturally to interests that burn hottest in the moment.  Drawn by interest and discovery.  No different than a kid finding an old favorite toy.

I didn't quit one for the other. I simply embraced the one that screamed loudest and gave it more attention.

In fact this whole short essay stems from a single personal insight from my notes:

>*Being drawn from one passion, only to find yourself in another is a whiplash inducing gift. A gift that is only given to those who allow themselves real, true joy.*

The fear. The self doubt. The anxiety. The pressure. All placed above me slowly lowering itself to crush me under an enormous weight. Put there with my own hands. 

All of these abilities are gifts.

My ability to curate the correct words. Describing complex emotions via a metorphical structure slowly inching it's way down with crushing menace. 

Evoking emotion with the tones I extract with fingers along the neck of a guitar. Giving noise character.

Reading a novel that takes place in world wholly unlike ours. Created by an artisan with such detail I can create scenes, splayed upon the pages, and play them as a movie would play, concealed in my head.

And the irony of it is I find myself receiving compliments on some of my works I don't feel I deserve. 

Not because I believe I'm bad at them. But because I'm not specialized in one of them.

But I'm not specialized, because a special talent hasn't erupted theough the surface with a enough force to drive me in a singular direction. I'm simply discovering and exploring what they are. With time on my side.
