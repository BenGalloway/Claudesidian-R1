William involuntarily set a quicker pace than anticipated on his walk to the rear door. Reaching his destination three and a half minutes earlier than expected. 

And, while it was a brisk night that required an extra outer layer of insulation, William didn't want to be early for his own termination. 

He waited.

Then entered through the familiar rear door, and proceeded down the similarly familiar steps. 

But this time he walked by sublevel 2 without so much as a glance on his dissent to 6. 

As he took the final downward step to the platform, he was greeted by yet another door. 

*It didn't say anything about another entrance to pass through.*

Glancing around the claustrophobic platform all he saw were the stairs that would return to his origin. And another set opposite those that continued downwards. 

*What would annailation effect sensory input?* Was the only question William could think about as he pushed through the door. 

On the other side of the door he found empty space. Illuminated by something embedded the ground. A color like that of the oceans all citizens were taught lapped the edges of their occupied land mass. Only, this shade was much brighter. And it was as if the room was condensating. As it was full of the tell tale fog, but bore none of the dampness. 

"Hello?" William was accustomed to these rooms containing something. The emptiness caused his chest to experience a tightness more significant than any before. 

He waited. 

And waited. 

And waited. 

And as he turned to open the door and remove himself from this room, he captured movement in the edges of his vision. 

Not sudden, but deliberate movement. Completing the revolution that was started to exit, he found himself starting at the pectorals of another citizen. 

Around the figure was the kind of formal wear designated for upper management and state officials. This one fitted like all the others, so well it almost seemed to be a second skin. And as William forced his eyes to meet the others, as the courtesy manual instructed he let out a faint, momentary gasp. 

He was not looking into the eyes of a citizen, but a rabbit. With ears standing at attention. Alerted. 

Only after William recovered from the sudden obscurity that evacuated all the oxygen from him did he realize that this was simply a citizen with a face covering in the shape of a rabbit. 

"I'm William, nice to meet you." He stuck out his hand. Unable to compensate for the tremors. 

The figure did not move. Made no attempt to to accept the courteous greeting. He simply demanded, "Tell me of your experience with the manual of truth."
